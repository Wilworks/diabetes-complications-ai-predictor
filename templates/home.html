The Problem We're Solving

Rising Global Crisis

Diabetes mellitus has become a global concern with rising prevalence. According to WHO, the mortality due to diabetes has been increasing steadily — in 2022, 14% of adults aged 18+ lived with diabetes, up from 7% in 1990. More than half (59%) of adults aged 30 and above living with diabetes were not taking medication.

Our AI Solution

The condition, caused by low or no insulin production from the pancreatic islets of Langerhans, is typically diagnosed using biomarkers such as Fasting Blood Glucose and HbA1c. However, these methods are often time-consuming and inefficient in detecting microvascular complications early — particularly in low-resource settings. Our research explores how AI can augment diagnostic accuracy, improve monitoring efficiency, and enable early prediction of complications like nephropathy, neuropathy, and retinopathy.
